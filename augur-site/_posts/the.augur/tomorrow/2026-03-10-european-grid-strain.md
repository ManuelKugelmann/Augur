## The Signal

European transmission system operators reported capacity margins below 5% across the Central-West
region for the third consecutive day. Wind generation across Germany and the North Sea dropped 40%
below seasonal averages as a blocking high-pressure system settled over Scandinavia. Meanwhile,
temperatures across Poland, Czech Republic, and Austria are running 6-8°C below the March norm,
driving heating demand well above forecast.

France's nuclear fleet, still recovering from the 2022-2024 maintenance backlog, has 8 of 56
reactors offline for scheduled refueling. Cross-border flows from France to Germany have reversed,
adding strain to an already tight system.

## The Extrapolation

If the blocking pattern holds through mid-March as meteorological models suggest, controlled
load-shedding becomes probable in at least one Central European grid zone by Thursday. Industrial
consumers on interruptible contracts will be curtailed first — but if cold persists into the weekend,
residential rolling outages of 1-2 hours become the contingency plan.

The political fallout will be immediate: energy ministers in Berlin, Vienna, and Prague will face
questions about the pace of grid storage buildout and the decision to retire baseload capacity
ahead of sufficient replacement.

## In The Works

The European Battery Alliance's 2025 gigafactory pipeline is now delivering: CATL's Erfurt plant
reached 14 GWh annual capacity in January, and Northvolt's Skellefteå facility ships grid-scale
cells to three German TSOs. Combined, new European grid storage commissioned in Q1 2026 totals
2.1 GW — a record, though still only 15% of what the IEA estimates is needed by 2030.

Austria's Kaprun pumped-hydro expansion (480 MW) completed commissioning in February and is
already cycling daily. It won't solve the immediate crisis, but it represents the kind of
long-duration storage that makes renewable-heavy grids resilient.
